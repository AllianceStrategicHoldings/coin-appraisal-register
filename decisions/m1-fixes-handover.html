What was addressed

Fix 1 — Numismatic / "times-face" pricing path

Coins like Wheat Pennies, Buffalo Nickels, Indian Head Pennies, and Costume Jewelry don't price by metal weight — they price as a multiple of face value. The app now supports this end-to-end:

  • Eleven new coin types added to the Add Coin picker, grouped under a new "Numismatic / Other" section at the bottom of the list.
  • The Airtable Config_CoinTypes table has a new fixed_multiplier column. The offer for each line is computed as quantity × face value × fixed_multiplier.
  • These items contribute to the Offer at Margin total but not to the 100% Melt total — by design, since they have no metal value.

Fix 2 — Numeric keypad on iPad quantity inputs

Every quantity and weight field on the iPad now opens a number-only keypad. The full alphanumeric keyboard never appears for these inputs. Weight fields (grams) include a decimal point; count fields (coins) are whole numbers only. This applies in both the Add Coin modal and when editing a quantity directly in the bag.

Fix 3 — Real-time dual totals (without tapping Calculate)

The header now shows two totals that update on every Add or quantity change — without requiring a Calculate Total tap:

  • 100% Melt — what the metal in the bag is worth at current spot prices.
  • Offer at Margin — what your policy says you'll pay for it.

Calculate Total still exists and still runs the authoritative server-side calculation — its result is shown as a green "Last calculated total" badge. The two header values give the rep an instant sense of where the deal sits as items are being added.

For each row, the offer paid per item is face_value × fixed_multiplier. For example, a Wheat Penny at face_value = 0.01 and fixed_multiplier = 5 pays $0.05 per coin. You set both numbers — there is no default.

2. Confirm the margin percentages in Config_Margins

Action: open the Config_Margins table in Airtable. All four rows currently read margin_pct = 0.9, which means the app pays 90% of melt for silver, gold, platinum, and collectibles. The original seed value was 0.30 (i.e., 30% of melt). Please confirm whether 0.9 is intentional before staff use this on the floor for a real customer.

The math is the same on both sides: offer = melt × margin_pct. So:

  • 0.30 → on $100 of silver melt, the offer is $30.
  • 0.90 → on $100 of silver melt, the offer is $90.

The change is yours to make in Airtable — no code change, no developer in the loop. The next Calculate Total on any iPad will use whatever values are saved when you save the row.
